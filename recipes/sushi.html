Sushi

Raw Fish, Rice, Seaweed

Description

Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish that features vinegared rice, often paired with raw or cooked seafood, vegetables, and sometimes tropical fruits. The most common types of sushi include nigiri, which are hand-pressed rice topped with fish, and maki, where the ingredients are rolled in seaweed (nori) and sliced into bite-sized pieces.

Served with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger, sushi offers a delicate balance of flavors, textures, and presentation, making it a global favorite for its fresh, minimalist approach to food. Variations range from simple, traditional forms to modern, fusion interpretations.

Ingredients

  • Raw Fish
  • Rice
  • Seaweed

Steps

  1. Lay Sheets of Seaweed flat on a cutting board
  2. Take piece of Raw Fish and roll it in sticky rice until fish is covered
  3. Place Fish with Rice onto sheet of Seaweed and roll it up, using extra grains of Rice to create a seal between the connecting pieces of Seaweed
  4. Enjoy your delicious Sushi
